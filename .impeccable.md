## Design Context

### Users
Primary audience is funders and institutional partners — grant makers, VCs, foundations, and academic/research institutions evaluating Praxor Lab's legitimacy and research output before committing capital, partnership, or institutional backing. Secondary readers are prospective research fellows and collaborators. Viewing context is desk-based, focused evaluation — not casual browsing. The site is a credibility instrument.

### Brand Personality
**Ambitious, precise, mechanical.** Confident builders. The work speaks. No hype, no marketing theater. Serious people writing seriously for serious readers.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark, aggressively de-clichéd. User explicitly asked to keep the current vibe and a modern sans aesthetic while stripping out the AI-lab tells.
- **Reference feel**: Editorial / scientific paper sensibility translated into a dark interface — think a well-made physics-lab instrument or the cover of a serious research quarterly, not a crypto or AI-startup landing page.
- **Anti-references (must NOT look like)**: The current monoculture of AI lab sites — cyan/purple/blue gradient accents, gradient text, glassmorphism-everywhere, floating orbs, particle networks, glow-on-hover buttons, DM Sans, Space Grotesk, Inter.
- **Type**: Keep a modern sans. Move away from DM Sans (reflex-listed). Pair with a mono for section numbering and technical metadata.
- **Color**: Drop the rainbow accent stack. One restrained signal color against a nearly-monochrome dark palette. Negative space and typography do the work.

### Design Principles
1. **The work speaks, not the chrome.** If a decorative element isn't serving legibility or hierarchy, it's cut. Funders read for signal, not spectacle.
2. **Typographic confidence beats visual effects.** Size, weight, and spacing establish hierarchy — not gradients, glows, or glassmorphism.
3. **Editorial cues over marketing cues.** Section numbering (§01), volume/date markers, ruled dividers, and measured columns — institutional, not promotional.
4. **One accent, used rarely.** A single signal color applied with restraint has more authority than a rainbow used everywhere.
5. **Dark, but not "AI dark."** Warm-tinted near-black, not pure #000; tinted neutrals, not slate-gray; ink-on-surface feel, not glowing panels.
